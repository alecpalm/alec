Orange Pod Update: Excused Absences, Accessibility, and Events

A Message from Your Orange Pod Representatives

Lewis & Clark Law Student Bar Association

Key Updates

  • You can always request an excused absence if you're sick by contacting Dean Libby Davis and completing the Excused Absence Request Form.
  • Accessibility concerns can be reported via DALSA or directly to student representatives.
  • Funds are available for Orange Pod events—share your ideas for Fall and Spring!

Hello, Orange Pod!

We attended the first SBA meeting of the year on Sunday and are excited to share key updates and opportunities with you.

1. Health and Safety on Campus

We raised concerns about the spread of illnesses on campus. If you're feeling unwell, you can request an excused absence by contacting Dean Libby Davis and completing the Excused Absence Request Form. The SBA is also working to provide more masks and other health resources. As a courtesy to your peers, please consider attending classes virtually when ill to keep our community healthy.

2. Accessibility Support

For accessibility concerns, you can submit a request through this form for DALSA (Disability Allied Law Student Association) to advocate on your behalf. Alternatively, contact our SBA accessibility representatives directly: Bethany Graves or Josephine Blatny. We're here to help facilitate communication or advocate for you, and we'll keep your information confidential, sharing only what you permit.

3. Pod Events and Funding

The SBA has allocated funds for Orange Pod events, and we want your ideas! We're planning one event this Fall and another in the Spring, with a potential cross-pod event in the works. Please reach out with your suggestions to make these events memorable.

These are the main highlights from the meeting. SBA meetings are open to all students, so feel free to join us at the next one! We're here to represent you, so please share your concerns or ideas—we're committed to ensuring your voices are heard.

Best,
Selyna Perez & Alec Palm
